**The 6-DOF Robotic Arm** is an exploration into industrial automation on a desktop scale.

While wheeled robots operate in 2D space, a robotic arm must conquer 3D space. This project replicates the kinematics of industrial robots (like KUKA or Fanuc), allowing for precise manipulation of objects, automated assembly tasks, and remote operation.

It isn't just about moving motors; it's about translating mathematical coordinates into physical motion.

## The Mathematics of Motion

Controlling a multi-jointed arm requires solving complex spatial geometry in real-time.

### Inverse Kinematics (IK)
The core challenge is **Inverse Kinematics**.
* **Forward Kinematics**: "If I set the elbow to 30° and wrist to 45°, where is the hand?" (Easy).
* **Inverse Kinematics**: "I want the hand at coordinate $(x, y, z)$ with a specific orientation. What angles do the motors need to be?" (Hard).

The onboard controller solves these trigonometric equations hundreds of times per second, allowing the end-effector to move in a straight line while all joints rotate simultaneously.

### Degrees of Freedom (DOF)
We utilized a **6-Axis Design**.
* **3 Axis** for Position ($X, Y, Z$).
* **3 Axis** for Orientation (Pitch, Roll, Yaw).
This allows the arm to approach an object from any angle, mimicking the flexibility of the human wrist and shoulder.

## Features

* **Computer Vision Integration**: Uses an OpenCV-based camera to identify, sort, and track objects by color or shape.
* **Teach & Repeat**: A manual "lead-through" mode where the user physically moves the arm, and it records the trajectory to replay later.
* ** Interchangeable End-Effectors**: Modular gripper design allows swapping between parallel claws, suction cups, or electromagnets.
* **Smooth Path Planning**: Implements acceleration and deceleration curves (S-curves) to prevent jerky movements and protect the gears.

## Components

* **Actuators (Base/Shoulder)**: NEMA 17 Stepper Motors (for high torque and precision).
* **Actuators (Wrist)**: MG996R Metal Gear Servos (for lightweight agility).
* **Controller**: Arduino Mega / ESP32 (handling IK calculations).
* **Drivers**: TB6600 Stepper Drivers.
* **Power**: 12V 30A Switching Power Supply.
* **Structure**: 3D Printed PETG with carbon fiber rod reinforcement.

## Challenges & Learning

The enemy of robotics is **gravity**.
The "Holding a milk jug" problem: The further the arm extends, the more torque is required at the base joint due to leverage. We solved this by adding **gas springs** and **counterweights** to the main joint, passively neutralizing the weight of the arm so the motors only have to lift the payload, not the arm itself.

## Use Cases

* **Automated Assembly**: Screwing, soldering, or pick-and-place operations.
* **Remote Manipulation**: Handling hazardous materials or objects in unreachable areas.
* **Education**: Visualizing coordinate systems, matrices, and control theory.
