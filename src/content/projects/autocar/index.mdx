**Autocar** is an experiment in "Camera-First" autonomy.

While our F1TENTH project uses lasers to map geometry, Autocar tries to drive like a human: using eyes. It relies entirely on a single front-facing camera to interpret the world, utilizing computer vision pipelines and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to understand lanes, traffic signs, and obstacles.

It is a study in how machines "see."

## The Vision Pipeline

Making a car drive itself using only pixels is a multi-step process.

### 1. Traditional Computer Vision (The "Rules")
For basic lane keeping, we use geometric algorithms:
* **Canny Edge Detection**: Converts the video feed into a high-contrast wireframe to find boundaries.
* **Hough Transform**: Mathematically finds lines within those edges to identify lane markers.
* **Perspective Transform**: Warps the image to a "bird's eye view" to calculate the curvature of the road ahead.

### 2. Behavioral Cloning (The "Brain")
For complex environments, we ditch the rules and use **Deep Learning**.
We implemented an **End-to-End Learning** model (inspired by NVIDIA's DAVE-2).
1.  We drive the car manually, recording video and steering angles.
2.  We feed this data into a CNN.
3.  The car learns to associate specific visual patterns (like a curve approaching) with specific actions (turning the wheel), effectively cloning the human driver's behavior.

## Features

* **Lane Keeping Assist**: Automatically centers the vehicle between white/yellow lines.
* **Traffic Sign Recognition**: Uses Haar Cascades or YOLO to detect Stop signs and Traffic Lights.
* **Emergency Braking**: Ultrasonic sensors act as a fail-safe to cut power if an obstacle is too close.
* **Shadow Resistance**: Advanced image thresholding to handle changing lighting conditions.

## Components

* **Compute**: Raspberry Pi 4 (4GB) or NVIDIA Jetson Nano.
* **Vision**: Raspberry Pi Camera Module V2 / wide-angle lens.
* **Motor Driver**: L298N H-Bridge.
* **Chassis**: 4WD Smart Robot Car Chassis.
* **Power**: 2x 18650 Li-ion Batteries.

## Challenges & Learning

The enemy of computer vision is **light**.
A code pipeline that works perfectly in a well-lit room often fails completely when a shadow falls across the track or the sun glares into the lens. We learned to use **HSV Color Space** instead of RGB, as it isolates color from lighting intensity, making the lane detection much more robust.

## Use Cases

* **ADAS Research**: Testing algorithms for Lane Departure Warning systems.
* **Last-Mile Delivery**: Prototyping delivery bots that stick to sidewalks.
* **Machine Learning Education**: Visualizing how neural networks "activate" when seeing a road.
